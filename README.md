# Onour Impram

> Clinical psychologist and AI researcher building accountable, privacy-first memory for AI systems.

My research and engineering circle one question. How do we build AI that remembers and reasons in ways we can audit, correct, and hold to account, especially where mental health and human wellbeing are at stake. Most of my open work lives at that seam, where rigorous systems engineering meets the duty of care that clinical training instills.

### Featured work

**[mneme](https://github.com/TheGoatPsy/mneme)** · Apache-2.0 · [site](https://thegoatpsy.github.io/mneme/)
Vault-native, accountable memory for Claude Code and MCP clients. Markdown is the source of truth, there is no LLM on the Stop path, and every derived store is redacted before it is written. Hybrid FTS5 and RRF retrieval, a temporal claim lifecycle with memory blame, gated knowledge-graph enrichment, and latency and quality benchmarks locked in CI.

**[claude-code-for-social-scientists](https://github.com/TheGoatPsy/claude-code-for-social-scientists)**
A bilingual guide, Turkish and English, that brings agentic coding to social scientists. Dual-licensed, with AI-use disclosure aligned to COPE, WAME, ICMJE, and the EU AI Act.
